The Meaning
Behind
BLACKPINK

The group’s name was conceived to contrast with the perception of femininity commonly associated with pink. When Blackpink debuted, their label YG Entertainment said the name intends to convey that the group embodies more than beauty. “I may look sweet, but I don’t act like it,” Jennie sings in the opening lines of “Ddu-DuDdu-Du,” echoing this message.

Meet the Members

LISA
@lalalalisa_m

Main Dancer, Lead Rapper

Lisa, the youngest Blackpink member from Thailand, started as a dancer before becoming a K-pop trainee. Her powerful and expressive dancing often goes viral in Blackpink's videos.


ROSÉ
@roses_are_rosie

Dancer

Rosé, born in New Zealand and raised in Australia, is fluent in English. She is the main singer of Blackpink, known for her distinct voice and ability to play guitar and piano.


 JENNIE
@jennierubyjane

Main Rapper, Lead Vocalist

Jennie, the longest trainee among the members, speaks fluent English, Korean, and Japanese. She was the first member to release a solo track, showcasing her versatility as a rapper and singer and establishing her as a fashion icon.


 JISOO
@sooyaaa__

Lead Vocalist, Visual

Jisoo, the oldest member of Blackpink, is known as the group's mood-maker. She has a unique sense of humour and a talent for spontaneous song-making. She acted in a number of music videos and commercials in her pre-Blackpink years.

The History of BLACKPINK


2016-2017: Debut and Early Success

Blackpink made their debut on August 8, 2016, with their single album " Square One." The album featured tracks "Boombayah" and "Whistle," showcasing their unique blend of hip-hop, pop, and EDM influences. The songs became instant hits, reaching number one on the Gaon Digital Chart. Blackpink's second album, "Square Two," in November 2016, solidified their popularity both domestically and internationally.

2018-2019: International Recognition and Breakthrough

In 2018, Blackpink embarked on their first world tour, " In Your Area," performing in various countries and captivating audiences with energetic performances. They collaborated with international artists like Dua Lipa on "Kiss and Make Up," expanding their global reach. In 2019, Blackpink became the first K-pop girl group to perform at the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival, showcasing their electrifying performances and gaining international attention. The EP "Kill This Love" was released in 2019, topping charts in multiple countries and breaking multiple records. The music video for "Kill This Love" became the most-watched music video within 24 hours on YouTube, surpassing 56.7 million views.

2020-2021: Global Domination and Iconic Achievements

In 2020, Blackpink achieved global success with their collaboration single " Sour Candy" with Lady Gaga. The album, "The Album," featured the chart-topping hit "Lovesick Girls" and broke records, becoming the highest-charting album by a female K-pop group on the Billboard 200. Blackpink also ventured into fashion and beauty, partnering with luxury brands like Chanel, Dior, and Bulgari. They became global ambassadors for high-profile campaigns, solidifying their status as style icons. In 2021, they collaborated with American singer Selena Gomez on "Ice Cream," which gained millions of views and streams worldwide. The group also released a documentary, "Blackpink: Light Up the Sky," on Netflix, providing an intimate look into their journey and behind-the-scenes moments.

2022-present: Current Status and Future Prospects

In July 2022, YG Entertainment announced Blackpink's final album recording and world tour plans. The group held a virtual in-game concert in PUBG Mobile, featuring performances of their hit songs and a special track called " Ready For Love." They also filmed a high-budget music video, surpassing previous production costs. In August 2022, Blackpink announced their project "Born Pink" and released a pre-release single, "Pink Venom." They also announced their second studio album, "Born Pink," set to be released in September. The BLACKPINK World Tour began on October 15 in Seoul, South Korea.
